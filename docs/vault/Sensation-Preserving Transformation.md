# Sensation-Preserving Transformation

A core mechanism of [[Model F]].

## The Principle

Input and output are equivalent in **felt quality** (affective tone) but different in **representational form**.

The **texture** of the signal is conserved. The **narrative** is generated.

## Example

Chest compression during sleep:
- **Input**: physical pressure on chest
- **Output**: nightmare involving conflict or suffocation scenario
- **Preserved**: the qualitative feeling of tightness, distress
- **Transformed**: the specific content (dream narrative)

The dream scenario, if experienced in waking life, would produce a feeling similar to chest compression. The [[Qualia|qualia]] is preserved across the transformation.

## In Model F

This maps to the [[Hormone System]] → [[Drive System]] → [[Impulse]] pipeline. The "texture" is the hormone profile (continuous physiological state). The "narrative" is the specific impulse that fires (discrete behavioral event). The affective quality is preserved through the weight matrix mapping.
